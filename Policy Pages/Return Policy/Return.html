Employing Cookies

The imprint a website may leave on users’ browsers during a visit is another issue that worries Internet users. The term “cookie” is frequently used to describe this imprint. “cookie” refers to a small amount of data a web server can save in your web browser’s cookie file and later retrieve. A web server must be aware that it can only access the cookie file placed on your computer and not access any other information on your hard drive. A web server cannot read cookie files other websites have put there, either. We use cookies to simplify the purchasing process on our website. Users’ browsers may also store and read “third-party cookies,” which are placed there by third parties. Only first-party cookies are covered by the privacy policy of IT Vision Networks Inc.

The “automatically accept cookies” default setting is commonplace for internet browsers. However, most browsers allow users to modify their settings to accept or reject cookies individually. Please consult your browser’s online help or the manufacturer’s website to change the cookies settings. However, if you choose to disable cookies, please be aware that some elements of our website might not be accessible to you, and some pages might not appear correctly. Visit Internet Cookies to learn more about cookies.
